## 🧪 The Core Formula (Per Batch)

This formula creates a **capillary-active, open-cell cement matrix** capable of transporting moisture and absorbing solar radiation.

### Ingredients
| Component | Quantity | Purpose |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| **Portland Cement** | 2 parts (by volume) | Structural binder |
| **Water** | As needed | Hydration agent |
| **Dawn Ultra Dish Soap** | 1/4 cup | Surfactant (creates micro-bubbles) |
| **Rubbing Alcohol (Isopropyl)** | 1 cup | Slurry base for Xanthan Gum |
| **Xanthan Gum** | 15g | Viscosity modifier (prevents slump) |
| **Activated Carbon (Optional)** | 10-15g | Creates Blackbody structure (95% absorption) |

### Preparation Steps

#### 1. Create the Xanthan Slurry
1. In a separate container, mix **15g Xanthan Gum** into **1 cup Rubbing Alcohol**.
2. Stir vigorously until fully dissolved. This prevents clumping when added to water.
   *Note: The alcohol helps disperse the gum without the "slime" texture of water-only mixes.*

#### 2. Prepare the Gel Base
1. In a large mixing bucket, combine **1/4 cup Dawn Ultra** with **~2 cups of water**.
2. Whisk gently until a uniform gel forms.
   *Target Consistency:* Thick, viscous, but pourable. It should hold a shape briefly before settling.
3. **Add the Xanthan Slurry** to the gel and mix thoroughly.
   *Result:* A non-slumping gel that suspends air bubbles effectively.

#### 3. The Critical Mix (Open-Cell Transition)
1. Add **2 parts Cement** to the gel base.
2. **Stir continuously** using a low-speed drill mixer or hand trowel.
3. **Observe the Bubbles:**
   - Initially, large bubbles will form and pop quickly.
   - **The Transition Point:** Continue stirring until you see a sudden volume of **microscopic bubbles** stabilizing on the surface and popping slowly when agitation stops.
   - **Visual Cue:** The mixture will look slightly "fluffy" or aerated, resembling a thick mousse.
   - **Stop Stirring** immediately once this transition occurs. Over-stirring collapses the cells; under-stirring leaves it closed-cell (no capillary action).

#### 4. Blackbody Modification (Optional but Recommended)
- If creating solar collectors: Add **10-15g Activated Carbon powder** during Step 3.
- Mix until the cement turns a deep, uniform matte black.
- *Physics Note:* This increases solar absorptivity from ~20% (gray cement) to ~95%, maximizing thermal gain.

## 🔬 Physics Validation: Why This Works

### 1. Capillary Action
The Xanthan Gum/Dawn gel creates a hydrophilic network within the cement pores. This allows water to wick through the material via capillary forces, enabling evaporative cooling without external pumps.

### 2. Micro-Sphere Strength
The surfactant (Dawn) traps air as microscopic spheres. These act as internal reinforcement, distributing stress and reducing cracking during thermal expansion/contraction cycles.

### 3. Open-Cell vs. Closed-Cell
- **Closed-Cell:** Bubbles are isolated. No fluid flow. Used for insulation.
- **Open-Cell:** Bubbles interconnect. Fluid (air/water) can flow through the matrix. **This is the target state for OpenCell.**
- *Validation Test:* Pour a small amount of water on a cured sample. If it absorbs within 30 seconds, the open-cell structure is successful.

## 🛠️ Application Guidelines

### Curing Process
1. **Mold:** Pour into forms (e.g., 5x13 ft panels or trench liners).
2. **Initial Set:** Cover with plastic to retain moisture for 24 hours.
3. **Hydration Cure:** Mist with water daily for 7 days. Do not let it dry out completely.
4. **Full Strength:** Reach 90% strength in 28 days.

### Thermal Performance Targets
- **Solar Absorption:** >95% (with Carbon)
- **Thermal Conductivity:** Optimized for heat transfer, not insulation.
- **Capillary Flow Rate:** ~0.5 L/m²/hr (dependent on pore size).

## 📝 Troubleshooting

| Issue | Cause | Fix |
|-------|-------|-----|
| **Too runny (Slump)** | Not enough Xanthan or too much water | Add more Xanthan slurry; reduce water in next batch. |
| **No bubbles / Dense** | Under-mixed or surfactant expired | Stir longer; ensure Dawn is fresh. |
| **Cracking** | Dried too fast | Improve curing humidity; add fiber mesh if needed. |
| **Weak structure** | Over-mixed (collapsed cells) | Stop stirring earlier at the "transition point." |
