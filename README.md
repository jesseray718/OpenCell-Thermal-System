# OpenCell Thermal-Mechanical Energy System

**Status:** Active Development | **License:** CC-BY-NC-SA 4.0 | **Version:** v1.1

A civilization-changing, open-source energy system providing zero-electric cooling and direct mechanical power for farms and homes. Built with **OpenCell Aerocement** and subterranean thermal exchange.

## 🌍 The Problem
Rural communities and small-scale farmers face three critical challenges:
1. **High Energy Costs:** Traditional grid reliance is expensive and fragile.
2. **Refrigeration Gaps:** Lack of affordable, zero-electric cold storage for produce.
3. **Mechanical Limitations:** Solar PV only solves electricity, not direct mechanical work (pumps, saws, grinders).

## 💡 The Solution: OpenCell
The OpenCell System bypasses inefficient electrical conversion for primary tasks. It uses a standardized **OpenCell Aerocement** matrix to capture solar heat, drive a Stirling/Rocket Mass engine, and leverage subterranean earth temperatures for passive refrigeration.

### Key Capabilities
*   **Zero-Electric Cooling:** 35°F output using 55°F earth heat sink.
*   **Direct Mechanical Power:** Belt-drive transmission for farm tools.
*   **Water Harvesting:** 24–48 gallons/day of fresh water via condensation.

## 🏗️ How It Works

### 1. The OpenCell Aerocement Collector
A vertical 5x13 ft panel made of a unique cement matrix infused with activated carbon.
*   **Mechanism:** Capillary action + Microscopic air bubbles (strength) + Blackbody absorption (95% solar capture).
*   **Formula:** See [`thermal-power-analysis.md`](./thermal-power-analysis.md) for the exact mixing protocol.

### 2. The Hybrid Power Train
Heat → Mechanical Work → (Excess) → Electricity.
*   **Engine:** Stirling/Rocket Mass integration.
*   **Transmission:** High-torque belt drive with safety slip.

### 3. The Subterranean Cold Loop
*   **Input:** Ambient air (70°F–100°F).
*   **Process:** Forced through a moist, airtight labyrinth lined with Aerocement.
*   **Output:** 35°F cooled, dehumidified air.
