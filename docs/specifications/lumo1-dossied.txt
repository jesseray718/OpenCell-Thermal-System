--- SECTION 1: CORE MATERIAL SCIENCE ---
INNOVATION: Open-Cell Aerocement.
CHEMISTRY/RECIPE:
- Base: Portland cement mixed at 1:2 ratio with gel slurry.
- Slurry: 15g Xanthan Gum in rubbing alcohol, mixed with 1/4 cup Dawn Ultra in water (near-zero slump gel).
- Mechanism: Mechanical stirring incorporates microscopic air bubbles. Transition point (bubbles popping) = open-cell porosity.
- Functionalization: Activated carbon added to gel creates volumetric blackbody absorber (alpha approx 0.95).
- Properties: Capillary-like cellular structure allowing gaseous/liquid exchange; freeze-thaw resistance potential.

--- SECTION 2: PHYSICS & THERMODYNAMICS ---
- Solar Input: S approx 800-1000 W/m^2 peak.
- Absorption: P_abs = alpha * A * S.
- Storage: Subterranean porous cement at ~10ft depth utilizing latent heat of fusion (water/ice).
- Energy Density: L approx 334 kJ/kg; Target >93 kWh per m^3 of saturated pore water.
- Flow Dynamics: Buoyancy-driven convective loop. Q ~ C * A_flow * sqrt(g * H * Delta_T / T).
- Pre-conditioning: Desiccant stage to manage humidity.

--- SECTION 3: COMPARATIVE REINFORCEMENT STUDY (THE GRANT CORE) ---
OBJECTIVE: Determine the superior system via head-to-head testing.
CANDIDATES:
A. Sodium Silicate (Silica Water): Chemical densification. Low cost, surface hardening only.
B. Classical Hex Mesh (Baseline): 27-gauge galvanized hex mesh. Proven technology, 2D reinforcement.
C. Alkali-Resistant (AR) Glass Fiber: Volumetric fibers mixed into slurry. Hypothesis: Mechanical interlock with microspheres creates superior 3D composite.

TEST PROTOCOL:
- Build 9 identical panels (3 per candidate).
- Tests: Compressive Strength (ASTM C39), Flexural Strength (ASTM C293), Impact Resistance, Freeze-Thaw (ASTM C666, 300 cycles).
- Decision: No winner declared until data proves superiority in flexural strength AND durability.

--- SECTION 4: CRITICAL BUILD SEQUENCE & CURING ---
1. Fabrication: Cut 4'x4' cardboard triangles.
2. Treatment: Soak in Acetone-Silicone solution.
3. Assembly: Click-flange geodesic sphere.
4. Infusion: Spray/trowel aerocement. (Add AR fibers here if Candidate C).
5. Mesh: Roll hex mesh (Candidate B only).
6. Shotcrete: 1:2 Cement/Sand, 0.4 w/c ratio.
7. CURING PROTOCOL (NON-NEGOTIABLE):
   - Start 4-6 hours after shotcrete.
   - Wrap in 6-mil plastic OR mist continuously.
   - DURATION: MINIMUM 21 DAYS.
   - Why: Full hydration required for design strength. Skipping reduces strength by 50%.
