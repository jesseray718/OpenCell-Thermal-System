## 4. The Joy Algorithm: Maximizing Happiness
Joy is not a random emotion. It is a **mathematical state**.

### 4.1 The Formula
$$ \text{Joy} = \frac{\text{Perceived Progress}}{\text{Expectation}} $$

- **If Expectation is High** (e.g., "I must finish the whole system today") and **Progress is Low**, Joy $\approx$ 0 (Frustration).  
- **If Expectation is Low** (e.g., "I will just fix one line of code") and **Progress is High**, Joy $\approx$ Max (Flow).  

### 4.2 The "Micro-Win" Strategy
To keep Joy high, you must **break the mountain into pebbles**.
1.  **Define a Micro-Goal:** "I will install `python` in Termux." (Not "I will build the whole app.")
2.  **Execute:** Do the thing.
3.  **Celebrate:** Say "Done." Feel the dopamine.
4.  **Repeat:** Move to the next pebble.

**The "Joy Log":**  
You must track your wins. If you don't see them, your brain forgets them.
