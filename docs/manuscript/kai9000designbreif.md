### 3. Spiral Internal Track – “Unlimited Draw Flow”  

1. **Spiral Track Geometry**  
   * The interior wall follows a **logarithmic spiral** defined by:  

   \[
   r(z) = r_0 \, \exp\!\Big(\frac{z}{p}\Big) \qquad
   \text{where } p = \phi D
   \]

   * \(r_0\) = radius at the base; \(z\) = height measured upward.  
   * The spiral pitch \(p\) ensures each turn is **φ‑times larger in radius** than the previous one, guaranteeing a **smooth, continuous curvature** with no sharp turns that would choke the flow.  

2. **Flow‑Enhancing Features**  
   * **Variable‑Pitch Helical Ribs**: 3‑mm‑high ribs spaced at intervals equal to \(p/3\) create **controlled vortex shedding** (Strouhal number ≈ 0.4) that boosts mixing without increasing drag.  
   * **Surface Roughness**: Engineered‑roughness at **5 µm RMS** stimulates early transition to turbulence, raising the **local Nusselt number** by ~30 % versus a smooth wall.  

3. **Turbulent Whirlpool/ Vortex Core**  
   * At the **mid‑height** (≈ 0.6 H) the spiral radius reaches a local minimum relative to the turn radius, generating a **self‑organising vortex ring** whose core diameter equals \(d_c\).  
   * This core ** draws fresh air upward continuously**, creating a **low‑pressure “suction”** at the base that drives passive intake without fans.  

4. **Inlet/Outlet Design**  
   * **Inlet**: Open circular aperture of area \(A_{\text{in}} = \frac{\pi D^2}{4}\).  
   * **Outlet (apex)**: Circular hole of area \(A_e\) calculated above. The exit hole is **flanged outward** to keep the jet aligned with the tower centreline and to prevent lateral turbulence that would bleed energy.  

5. **Bottleneck Prevention**  
   * The **exit velocity** is limited to  

   \[
   v_{\text{out}} = \sqrt{\frac{2 \Delta P_{\text{total}}}{\rho}} \le 1.5 \, v_{\text{in}}
   \]

   * This guarantees the **back‑pressure does not exceed the inlet driving pressure**, ensuring a **steady, unlimited draw** even under low‑wind conditions.

### 8. Construction & Assembly Blueprint  

1. **Foundation** – A shallow **reinforced‑concrete ring** (diameter = 0.9 × tower base) poured on a **gravel‑drainage layer** to prevent water stagnation.  

2. **Geodesic Shell Fabrication** –  
   * Prefabricated **ferrocement panels** (≈ 30 cm thick) are **segment‑molded** in a factory using a **φ‑based mold grid**.  
   * Panels are **bolted together on‑site** using stainless‑steel **tension rings** that maintain the geodesic geometry, eliminating the need for complex curved formwork.  

3. **Spiral Waterway Installation** –  
   * The interior **spiral track** is formed by **poured concrete ribs** following the logarithmic‑spiral equation.  
   * Ribs are **tapered** from 5 cm at the base to 2 cm at the apex, ensuring minimal obstruction to flow.  

4. **Vortex‑Enhancing Ribs** –  
   * **3‑mm stainless‑steel ribs** are welded to the spiral wall at intervals of \(p/3\). Their **height** follows a sine‑wave envelope to maximise vortex shedding without generating excessive drag.  

5. **Apex Turbine Assembly** –  
   * A **direct‑drive, 1‑kW permanent‑magnet generator** is mounted on a **flexible, vibration‑damped flange** that isolates the turbine from tower resonance.  
   * The **exit nozzle** is a **convergent‑divergent diffuser** shaped to the calculated \(A_e\) and coated with a **hydrophobic finish** to prevent water‑film buildup that could choke the flow.  

6. **Water‑Harvest Coating & Collection** –  
   * The outer shell receives a **spray‑applied TiO₂‑nanocoated hydrophilic layer**. Condensed water flows down **smooth‑lined gutters** into the ferrocement tank via **gravity‑driven channels** with no pumps.  

7. **Modular Coupling** –  
   * **Standardised steel‑to‑steel couplers** (ISO‑compatible) allow any node to **plug into the next** while maintaining structural integrity and fluid continuity.  
   * Electrical connectors are **IEC‑62196‑2 compliant** for plug‑and‑play power linking.  

8. **Commissioning** –  
   * **Flow‑balancing test**: verify inlet/outlet velocity ratio < 1.5.  
   * **Water‑condensation test**: confirm ≥ 200 L day⁻¹ collection at 30 °C/70 % RH.  
   * **Power‑output test**: load a calibrated resistive bank and record steady‑state kW.
